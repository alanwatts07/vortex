# Vortex — Business Plan (working draft)

_Status: pre-seed / prototype. This is a living draft — numbers marked
**[assumption]** or **[verify]** are placeholders to replace with real data
before any investor conversation._

> **Vortex** is a location-based *presence* app. Flip your light on to become a
> colored dot on a radar — a quiet signal that you're open to connect, right
> here, right now. You find each other in person, by color. Not a dating app,
> not a profile app: no bios, no swiping, no chat. The color is the introduction.

See [Plans.md](./Plans.md) for the product roadmap and principles this plan
builds on.

## Direction (locked with founder)

- **Ambition:** a real, scalable startup.
- **Model:** dual revenue — **venues/events pay (B2B)** + **free-with-premium**
  for individuals.
- **First market beyond founder's karaoke nights:** **festivals & conferences.**
- **Funding:** bootstrapping the prototype, **open to investors** to accelerate.

## 1. The problem

Being around people is not the same as being *reachable*. In a bar, a festival,
a conference — you're surrounded by people who might be great to meet, but the
social cost of approaching a stranger is high and the signal of "I'm actually
open to this" is invisible. Existing tools make it worse:

- **Dating apps** reduce people to profiles and swipes, are romance-framed, and
  pull connection onto a screen and out of the room.
- **Social/event apps** are about broadcasting *after* the fact, not enabling a
  face-to-face moment *in* the fact.

There's no low-stakes, in-person, "I'm open right now" signal. That's the gap.

## 2. The product

One gesture: a switch. Turn it on and you appear as a colored dot on a radar of
others nearby who are also open. Your color is your **aura** — a self-chosen
energy from a field of drifting colors. You recognize each other in the real
world by color ("are you the teal one?"). Turn it off and you vanish — no trace.

**Core principles (the moat and the brand):** no logins or profiles; presence is
ephemeral; location only while your light is on; the app *ends at the
introduction* — the meeting happens face to face. These aren't limitations;
they're the product's point of view, and they're hard for a profile-based
incumbent to copy without contradicting themselves.

## 3. Why now

- Ubiquitous phones with precise location and always-on data.
- Real-time presence infra is cheap and off-the-shelf (no custom backend to
  start).
- Post-pandemic hunger for genuine, in-person connection and a cultural backlash
  against profile/swipe fatigue.
- "Anti-app app" positioning (less screen, more life) is resonant right now.

## 4. Go-to-market — density is everything

**The one existential risk:** a presence app is worthless until enough people
are on *in the same place at the same time*. Launch "everywhere" and everyone
sees an empty radar and leaves. So we never sell "an app" — we light up
**bounded scenes** where density is manufactured, not hoped for.

**Phased wedge:**

1. **Proof (now): founder's karaoke nights.** A recurring, host-controlled room.
   The host has a mic and can make the whole crowd flip on at once. Weekly
   feedback loop with real humans. Zero CAC. Proves the core loop is *fun*.
2. **First scalable market: festivals & conferences.** Huge, bounded,
   co-located crowds = instant density, the best possible showcase of a full,
   colorful radar. Event organizers are a clean B2B buyer. Great for
   **acquisition and proof**.
   - _Known tradeoff:_ festivals are **seasonal and one-off**, so they drive
     spikes, not retention. We deliberately pair them with recurring formats
     (karaoke → nightlife) so users have a reason to open vortex *between*
     festivals. Festivals fill the top of the funnel; recurring venues keep it.
3. **Recurring nightlife: bars & events.** Same "host owns the room" playbook as
   karaoke, repeatable venue-by-venue. This is the retention and density engine.
4. **Campuses / communities**, then eventually **ambient/global** presence once
   the behavior is proven and density is self-sustaining.

**Growth loop:** nail one scene → package it as a repeatable playbook (a QR/code
to join a scene, host kit, signage) → roll venue-by-venue and event-by-event.
Adoption is host-led, not a global cold launch.

**Make the color leave the screen.** Pins, stickers, wristbands, lock-screen
colors in the aura hues — so recognition happens in the physical world and the
brand lives offline. (Also a merch/partnership surface — see revenue.)

## 5. Business model (dual)

**A. Venues & events pay (B2B) — primary near-term revenue.**
- A festival, conference, bar, or venue pays to run a **branded vortex scene**
  for their crowd (a private/space-scoped radar, their name/colors, join QR,
  host controls, and basic analytics on engagement).
- Pricing shapes to explore: per-event fee for festivals/conferences

  **[assumption: $Y/month]**.
- Why it fits: keeps the app **100% free and clean for people** — no ads, no
  selling data — which protects the ethos while the people who profit from
  connection (venues, organizers) pay for it.

**B. Free-with-premium (consumer) — secondary, later layer.**
- Core experience always free. Optional paid perks that never compromise the
  no-profile ethos: e.g. wider range, extra aura colors/effects, "beacon" to
  help someone find you in a crowd, personal (private) aura history.

  **[assumption: Z% convert at $W/mo]**.

**C. Adjacent (optional, later):** merch (aura pins/wristbands), event
partnerships/sponsorships. Kept secondary so incentives stay aligned with users.

## 7. Competition & differentiation

- **Dating apps (Tinder, Hinge, etc.):** romance-framed, profile/swipe-based,
  on-screen. Vortex is explicitly *not* that — no profiles, no romance framing,
  meeting is in person. Different intent, different moment.
- **Proximity/social apps (past attempts):** most failed on **cold-start**
  (empty radar) by launching globally. Our scene-scoped, host-led density model
  is the direct answer to why they died.
- **Event apps:** logistics/schedules, not spontaneous human connection.

**Defensibility / moat:**
- **Local network effects per scene** — each lit-up venue/event is its own dense
  network that's hard to displace once it's the norm there.
- **Host & organizer relationships** — B2B distribution that compounds.
- **Brand as ritual** — "flip your light on," the color mechanic, offline color
  presence. A point of view competitors can't copy without abandoning their own.
- **The ethos itself** — a profile-based incumbent can't credibly ship "no
  profiles, ephemeral, meet in person."

## 8. Milestones & metrics

**Phase 0 — Prove the loop (now → next few months).**
- Run vortex live at karaoke nights repeatedly.
- _Metric:_ % of a room that flips on when prompted; do strangers actually meet
  because of it? (qualitative + simple counts). This is the make-or-break signal.

**Phase 1 — First paid pilots (festivals/conferences).**
- Land 1–3 festival/conference pilots; ship the "scene" product (join QR, host
  controls, basic analytics) + background presence groundwork.
- _Metrics:_ concurrent lit users per scene, connections made, organizer
  willingness to pay / renew.

**Phase 2 — Repeatable playbook (recurring nightlife).**
- Sign recurring venues; prove venue-by-venue repeatability and retention
  between events.
- _Metrics:_ venues live, weekly active scenes, MRR, retention curve.

**Phase 3 — Scale & (if raising) seed round.**
- Expand metros/markets; hire; formalize the B2B sales motion.

**North-star metric:** connections made in person per scene (density × intent),
not raw downloads.

## 11. Key risks & mitigations

- **Cold-start / density:** _the_ risk → scene-scoped, host-led launches;
  festivals for instant density; recurring venues for retention.
- **Retention between events:** festivals spike then fade → pair with recurring
  nightlife/karaoke so there's always a live scene to come back to.
- **Safety** (real-time location + strangers): location jitter, in-session
  block/report, auto-expiry, and bounded/vetted crowds (events) which are easier
  to trust and moderate. Must be built before any broad launch.
- **Background presence needs a native app:** known, scoped work (Capacitor wrap
  + geofence/heartbeat + auto-expire), deferred deliberately so the web version
  stays testable now.
- **Monetization timing:** B2B (events pay) is validated earliest; consumer
  premium layered later so we don't tax early density.
- **Platform/App Store:** background "always" location gets scrutiny → use
  geofencing/region monitoring, which is the review-friendly path.
